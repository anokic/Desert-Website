Cats were represented In religious practices

Cats in ancient Egypt were represented in social and religious practices of Ancient Egypt for more then 30 centuries. Several Ancient Egyptian deities were depicted and and sculptured with cat-like heads such as Mafdet, Bastet and Sekhmet, representing justice, fertility and power The deity Mut was also depicted as a cat in the company of a cat.
